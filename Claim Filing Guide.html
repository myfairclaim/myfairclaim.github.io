5-Step Claim Filing Process

Follow these steps in order for maximum success rate

1 Gather Your Documentation

Before you begin, collect these documents:

  • Enhanced Diminished Value Report - Attached to your confirmation email
  • Vehicle Valuation Report - Attached to your confirmation email
  • Repair invoices - All repair bills and receipts
  • Pre-accident photos - If available (helpful but not required)
  • Post-repair photos - Current photos of your vehicle
  • Insurance policy documents - Your policy number and coverage info

4 Submit Your Claim (Dual Method Recommended)

Best Practice: Use BOTH methods for maximum impact

Method A: Email Submission (Fast)

  • Use the "Generate Email" tab to create an email version
  • Send to your adjuster's email address
  • Attach both PDF reports from your confirmation email
  • Advantage: Immediate delivery, easy to track

Method B: Certified Mail (Legal Proof)

  • Print your demand letter
  • Include physical copies of both reports
  • Send via USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt
  • Cost: ~$10 | Processing: 3-5 business days
  • Advantage: Creates legal proof of submission date
‚úì Why Both? Email gets your claim to the adjuster immediately. Certified mail creates a legal record and shows you're serious. Insurance companies respond faster when they see both.
5 Track, Document & Follow Up

Timeline & Actions:

Days 1-7: Initial Review Period

  • Insurance company acknowledges receipt (usually within 3 days)
  • Claim assigned to adjuster
  • Initial review begins
  • Your action: Wait for acknowledgment, document receipt date

Days 8-14: First Follow-Up

  • If no response, call or email adjuster
  • Ask for: claim status, timeline for decision, any questions
  • Document all communication (date, time, person spoken to, what was said)

Days 15-30: Decision Period

  • Most claims receive decision within 2-3 weeks
  • Follow up weekly if no decision
  • If denied or lowball offer: You have options (see our Premium tier tools)
